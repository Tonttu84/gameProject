Movement speed / retreat cost: currently all units move 1 hex per tick in any
direction. Retreating while in contact with an enemy should be slower or costlier
than advancing — historically backing away under pressure was dangerous and tiring.
Needs a speed/movement-points system before this is expressible. Consider adding a
per-unit movementSpeed field (default 1) so faster units and penalties can be layered
on top without reworking all movement logic at once.

Accuracy system — needs design review:
  Currently accuracy is a 0-100 percentage used by ranged units for two things:
    1. Aimed range: accuracy/10 hexes — within this distance the unit can attempt
       to target an individual rather than just a hex.
    2. Aimed shot success: getRandom(1,100) <= accuracy — human archer at 50 has
       50-50, a gaze/mind-blast at 99 almost always picks its target.
  Open questions:
    - Should accuracy also affect the deviation of the shot (Deviate currently uses
      dist/accuracy but with hex distances the integer division usually gives 0,
      so it does nothing useful at the moment)?
    - Should melee units have a meaningful accuracy stat, or is it only for ranged/special?
    - The default accuracy=10 on AUnit means any unit with a special() that does a
      targeted attack gets 10% aimed chance and 1-hex aimed range — is that a sensible
      default or should it be 0 (no aimed shot) until explicitly set?

Armor fatigue: heavier armor should increase fatigueCost in combat (and possibly MARCHFATIGUE for
marching). Currently all unit fatigue costs are flat — armor type should be a multiplier or addend
on fatigueCost so plate > chain > leather > none. Soldier already has +1 heavy armor penalty as a
start; generalize this across all unit/armor types when adding more variety.

Historical archer mechanics (not yet implemented):
  - Morale suppression from volleys: arrow hits should inflict a temporary morale penalty
    on all units in the target hex, not just kill points. Historically the psychological
    effect of a volley was often larger than the casualties.
  - Forced advance under fire: units taking arrow fire should have a chance to fail a
    discipline check and advance early, breaking formation (Agincourt-style — French knights
    charged through mud because they couldn't endure the arrow storm).
  - Skirmisher behaviour: archers should automatically try to maintain a minimum distance
    from approaching enemies and retreat when melee units close in, baiting charges then
    falling back.
  - Volley bonus for massed fire: multiple archers targeting the same hex should multiply
    the morale suppression effect. A lone archer is irritating; a hundred is terrifying.
